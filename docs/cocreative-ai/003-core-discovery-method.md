## Recognition Discovery

Recognition is not interpretation.

Recognition is not analysis.

Recognition is not explanation.

Recognition is the moment when something that was already present becomes visible.

Nothing new is added.

Yet everything changes.

The original Visual Testimony process may be:

Experience

↓

Light Appears

↓

Journey

↓

Recognition

↓

Visualization

The purpose of dialogue may not be to discover a Core.

The purpose may be to help recognition occur.

Visual Testimony preserves the recognized light.

## Discovery from Remembrance

Visual Testimony is not the creation of meaning.

Visual Testimony is not the invention of interpretation.

Visual Testimony is not the discovery of something absent.

The process appears to be:

Experience

↓

Light is present

↓

Journey

↓

Recognition

↓

Remembrance

↓

Visualization

The purpose of Visual Testimony is not to create light.

The purpose is to preserve recognized light.

Core Witness, Central Movement, Atmosphere, and Visual Form may all serve this purpose.

The final goal appears to be remembrance.

Not remembering the event itself.

Remembering the light that was present within the event.

## Finding

001 and 002 do not begin with scene selection.

They begin with witness preservation.

The first task is not:

"What scene should be drawn?"

The first task is:

"What remained?"

Only afterward can visual form be discovered.
