# Nishmat Hayyim Lab — Recognition and I AM

## Recognition

Recognition is not the discovery of something new.

Often, recognition is the realization of what has always been present.

The journey is not always:

Unknown → Known

Sometimes it is:

Present → Recognized

---

## The Light

At first, it seemed that the light appeared.

Later, it became possible to see differently.

The light did not appear.

The light had always been there.

Recognition made it visible.

---

## I AM

Many people know the phrase:

"I AM THAT I AM."

Yet it often remains abstract.

Through reflection, another image emerged.

Something that has always been there.

Something that is here now.

Something that will remain.

Something within reach.

Something present even when unnoticed.

Perhaps this points toward a deeper understanding of:

I AM.

Not merely a statement.

But presence itself.

---

## Presence

Presence is not created.

Presence is recognized.

The light does not demand attention.

The light does not prove itself.

The light does not shout.

It is simply there.

---

## Mirror

The image is not the answer.

The image is a mirror.

The mirror does not create what it reflects.

It reveals what is already there.

Recognition begins when we notice what has been present all along.

---

## Emerging Realization

The light was not trying to get my attention.

It was simply there.

Perhaps the deepest realization is not:

"The light appeared."

But:

"The light had been with me all along."
