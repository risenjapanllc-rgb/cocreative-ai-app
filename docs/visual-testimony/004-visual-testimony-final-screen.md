# Visual Testimony Final Screen

## Purpose

The purpose of the final screen is not to communicate information.

It is not to maximize engagement.

It is not to encourage consumption.

The purpose is to create a space where stillness becomes possible.

A place where seeing may emerge.

A place where remembrance may awaken.

---

## Experience Flow

Arrival
↓
Stillness
↓
Presence
↓
Seeing
↓
Remembrance

The screen should not rush the user toward content.

It should invite the user into a slower rhythm.

---

## First Impression

The user does not encounter a story first.

The user encounters silence.

A quiet visual field.

A sense of depth.

A subtle light.

The screen should feel less like an article and more like entering a small sanctuary.

---

## The Light Beneath

The central visual motif is not a flame.

The image originates from a dream:

A light discovered beneath the water.

This light represents the quiet discovery that emerges in stillness.

It is not imposed from above.

It is found in the depths.

---

## Layout Principles

### 1. Space before Content

Generous whitespace is essential.

Silence is part of the testimony.

The empty space is not absence.

It is invitation.

---

### 2. Presence before Explanation

A short sentence may appear before any testimony.

Examples:

"A light remained."

"He was already there."

"I could finally see."

These are not explanations.

They are openings.

---

### 3. Seeing before Story

The testimony itself is secondary.

The story may be collapsed initially.

The screen first invites reflection.

The story is available when needed.

---

### 4. Remembrance before Action

The final movement is inward.

Not:

"Read more"

But:

"What do you see now?"

"What remains?"

"Where is the light?"

The screen gently returns the experience to the visitor.

---

## Design Qualities

Quiet

Deep

Unhurried

---

## Core Principle

Visual Testimony is not a place to read testimonies.

It is a sanctuary where a quiet light awakens remembrance.
