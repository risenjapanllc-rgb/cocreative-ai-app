# The Light Was Always There

## The Dream

The journey began in darkness.

Not fear.

Not chaos.

A deep and quiet darkness.

Within that darkness, a small light appeared.

At first it was barely noticeable.

A presence more than an object.

Something that simply existed.

---

## Notice

The first moment was not understanding.

It was noticing.

"A light."

Nothing more.

No explanation.

No certainty.

Only awareness.

---

## Focus

As attention rested on the light,
it seemed to become larger.

Not because the light changed.

Because attention changed.

The light became clearer.

More real.

More present.

---

## Reach

The next movement was spontaneous.

A hand reached toward the light.

Not out of obligation.

Not out of instruction.

Out of desire.

The light invited response.

---

## Receive

The moment the light was touched,
everything changed.

Not with an explosion.

Not with violence.

Not with force.

The light simply expanded.

Softly.

Naturally.

Completely.

---

## The Disappearance of Darkness

The darkness did not seem to be defeated.

It simply disappeared.

The light filled everything.

What once appeared small
was revealed to be immeasurably larger.

The light had always been greater.

It had only appeared small from a distance.

---

## A Pattern for Visual Testimony

The experience follows a pattern:

Notice
↓
Focus
↓
Reach
↓
Receive

This is not only a dream.

It is the journey of seeing.

The journey of remembrance.

The journey back to the first love.

---

## The Light Did Not Change

The light did not become brighter.

The observer drew nearer.

The light did not grow.

Its true magnitude became visible.

---

## Remember the First Love

To remember the first love is not to recover a lost feeling.

It is to discover that the light was present from the beginning.

The love was always there.

The scales simply had not fallen yet.

---

## Visual Testimony

Visual Testimony is not a place to consume stories.

It is a place to notice the light.

To draw near.

To reach.

To receive.

And finally,

to discover that the light was always greater than the darkness.
